13th September
Yesterday we were briefed for 'Sequential Sketchbook' project. The idea amazes me. I can't stop thinking of graphic design workshop at Letnyaya Shkola and keep track of possible exercises. This one may become a foundation for an everyday image-making practice.

The ‘Sequential Sketchbook’ is about responding visually or conceptually to the image from the previous day. The first image may be everything you would like it to be, from the most favorite photograph to the exquisite flower. Throughout this everyday practice you gradually develop the idea and watch it transforms. Mind is an unlimited source of creative energy.

28th September
Art is a habit. It's not a talent, not a flame inside, not a gift, but a habit. I once thought that 'real artists' cannot stop creating. Poetic and romanticised construct — artist as a natural born destiny. Now I see: you either have a habit to turn your experiences into a form of art, or you don't. This is the only difference.

Here's Anton Reva who had developed an approach to imagery processing during one year in Saint Martins College, and kept working with conveyer persistence, and eventually turned into a machine for analogue experiments.

Here's me, who was drawing on a daily basis till 18 years old, until I started my college education. Also I was keeping a diary and wanted to write novels based on this experience. I thought of myself as of a writer just because I was indeed writing. Because I had a habit to do it all the time.

If you treat art as a habit, the only thing you have to do now is to bring this habit back to your routine. And apply all the newfound healthy artistic approaches to it.

7th October
This Saturday I finally bought an inkjet printer that doesn't need cartridges to top-up ink. It feels amazing to earn money by yourself and spend it the way you want.

Yesterday I was experimenting with two types of paper. Water-soluble ink flows and smears exactly the way I want. Cartridge paper sustains more manipulations, but writing paper shines through due to its thinness, which allows to work with the back side of the sheet.

I like the idea to print on paper made for different purposes. For example: baking paper, receipt paper, instructions, book pages, sticker notes, cotton paper, tracing paper, packages, palettes, posters, advertisement, cash. I definitely have to start collecting all these papers in a special box.

My main plan for tomorrow is to come up with a list of materials, paper kinds, and techniques, and try something new everyday while working on Sequential Sketchbook.

16th October
I grow more and more interested in the phenomena of sloth and idleness in the context of art. What is laziness in creativity? It seems like unwillingness to make effort to extract something from yourself. Because usually people are focused on extraction. One cannot just relax and write a short simple text. You feel obliged to give birth to a big and elaborated piece of work. This is the moment where fear and procrastination emerge—too much responsibility is always terrifying.

All these maximalist mindsets lead to artistic idleness. Creation is fun! This is the most important principle to learn. This is the idea to preserve and nurture in people after childhood. In the beginning you are fuelled by your natural child's spontaneity, but as a teenager you should raise a conceptual foundation in yourself to keep creating freely and with pure joy.

23rd October
Today I was occupied with collaging for sequential sketchbook for the whole day. Bad news: I work for the process, not the result, paying more attention to exploration of new techniques rather than creating finished works. Good news: I know that I don't like working with the material I've already used for any other purposes. If only I undertook a photo-shooting specifically for collaging, everything would have been different. But thereby I revealed a new reason to take more and better photos. God knows how much I love analogue processing.

I started working with found paper and plan to continue. This is the sweetest part of my work: to look how 110 year old tracing paper would be affected by water; to smear modern printer paint over soviet glossy album book sheets; to work with back side of an image, creating a collage on a thin writing paper.

Old paper adds value to my prints—in this way they become truly unique. Paper is everything. The whole magic of the process depends on a paper. It behaves differently, unpredictably each and every time. I am endlessly curious. Now I perfectly imagine the motivation of going to old abandoned paper factories to find precious wastes.
